Golden, crispy taco shells filled with juicy seasoned ground beef, fresh iceberg lettuce, melted cheddar cheese, and a spicy tomato salsa. These homemade beef tacos are the perfect combination of crunchy, savory, and fresh. Inspired by classic Tex-Mex flavors, this easy recipe is ideal for a quick weeknight dinner, a casual party, or a fun family meal. Ready in around 30 minutes, the tacos can also be customized with your favorite toppings like sour cream, jalapeños, avocado, or fresh cilantro.

ingredients for 12 tacos:

  • 1 tbsp oil
  • 1 onion
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 500g lean ground beef
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 0.5 tsp chilli powder
  • 1 tsp paprika powder
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp ground coriander
  • 0.5 tsp oregano
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 12 small taco shells
  • 4 large iceberg lettuce leaves, chopped
  • 190g grated cheddar cheese
  • 100g tomato salsa
  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (top/bottom heat).
  2. Heat the oil in a large pan over low heat. Cook the onion until soft but not browned. Add the garlic and sauté for 1 minute. Stir in the tomato paste, chili powder, paprika powder, cumin, coriander, oregano, sugar, and 1 tsp salt. Cook over medium heat for about 3-4 minutes. Turn the heat to high, add the ground beef, and brown it well. Add about 3 tbsp water. The pan should be hot enough for most of the liquid to evaporate quickly. Stir the mixture constantly for 5-10 minutes. If the meat starts sticking to the pan, add a little more water.
  3. Warm the taco shells in the preheated oven for 5 minutes.
  4. Serve the beef mixture with the taco shells, iceberg lettuce, cheddar cheese, and tomato salsa, allowing everyone to fill their own tacos at the table.
